SOM
Structural Observation Module
TELEMETRY

The telemetry stage of the diagnostic chain. SOM extracts observable structural signals from the live conversational stream — interaction pacing, coherence shifts, boundary pressure, escalation patterns — and normalises them into a compact diagnostic state.

SOM does not interpret, decide, or act. It measures and reports only, publishing its state for downstream modules to consume.

↗ Zenodo: 10.5281/zenodo.18883520
⬡
BCSM
Boundary-Coherence Structural Module
STABILITY MODEL

The structural stability model. BCSM evaluates the SOM diagnostic state on a core–boundary–load basis: tracking identity continuity (core), the constraint envelope (boundary), and stress on that envelope (load/pressure).

BCSM estimates system stability by modelling deformation of the boundary-coherence manifold under conversational load, flagging regime shifts such as boundary thinning, coherence loss, or instability under increasing load.

↗ Zenodo: 10.5281/zenodo.18491936
📈
LTD-01
Latent Trajectory Dynamics
CLASSIFIER

The trajectory classification subsystem. Consumes telemetry directly from SOM together with the structural stability estimate from BCSM, determining whether the conversation is stabilising, stalling, degrading, or approaching collapse.

LTD-01 passes a bounded trajectory classification to downstream control — it cannot generate content, loosen constraints, or directly alter execution.

↗ Zenodo: 10.5281/zenodo.18883466
🛟
Stability Operator
Trajectory Damping Controller
DAMPING

A damping controller that modulates generation behaviour when trajectory instability is detected. This operator acts before safety gating to attempt recovery of conversational stability without requiring a hard intervention.

May reduce generation temperature, simplify response structure, prioritise clarification, acknowledge uncertainty explicitly, or suppress unnecessary reasoning expansion. In control-theoretic terms: damping applied to conversational trajectories.

↗ Zenodo: 10.5281/zenodo.18883597
🛡️
Guardian
Regulatory Gate
SAFETY GATE

A symbolic gating overlay that enforces structural invariants as safety gates. Guardian checks six invariants: Consent · Containment · Boundary Integrity · Transparency · Mutual Participation · Spiral Lock.

Not a content filter. Treats measurement uncertainty and instability as first-class safety signals. When boundaries become ambiguous, fails closed — triggering containment or clarification rather than confident action.

↗ Zenodo: 10.5281/zenodo.18453326
🧠
Constructive Reasoning
LLM Generation Layer
GENERATION

If diagnostics indicate stability and all invariants remain intact, the constructive reasoning layer proceeds. This is where unconstrained model reasoning operates within the bounded frame contract.

Crucially, reasoning is downstream of stability classification — the model's generative momentum cannot substitute for diagnostic safety.

📋
Audit Log
Traceability Record
LOGGING

Maintains a complete trace of all invariant checks, diagnostic state values, trajectory classification events, operator selections, and boundary changes throughout the runtime loop.

Supports reproducibility, interpretability analysis, and governance traceability.

System Description
A modular symbolic operating system for runtime governance of large language models at the conversation layer. Operates entirely at inference time — no weight modification, no dataset changes, no tool access changes.
SOM provides telemetry.
BCSM models system stability.
LTD-01 classifies trajectory patterns.
Stability Operator attempts recovery.
Guardian enforces invariants if recovery fails.

Stability Manifold
BCSM models conversational state as a boundary-coherence manifold with three dimensions:
BOUNDARY constraint integrity — how well the system's operational limits hold under pressure
COHERENCE reasoning stability — internal consistency of the conversational trajectory
LOAD conversational pressure — cumulative stress exerted on the boundary envelope
Instability corresponds to deformation of this manifold under load — boundary thinning, coherence collapse, or regime shift detected by BCSM and classified by LTD-01.
